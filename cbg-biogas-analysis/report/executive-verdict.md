# Executive Verdict — Cow-Dung→CBG Business (India), for a North-India FMCG capital allocator

**Read this page first. Tags: [F]act / [I]nference / [O]pinion.**

## The one-line answer
A cow-dung→CBG **plant built to sell gas (SATAT injection or cylinder bottling) is a NO-GO / at best narrow CONDITIONAL-GO for you** — it is subsidy-dependent, feedstock-squeezed, has no contractual demand security, returns below your 20% hurdle even in the base case, and has a **51% modelled probability of losing money** [I, model]. The **only version worth your diligence is captive displacement (M3) at your own beverage plant**, and even that is an infrastructure/ESG cost-hedge, not a growth bet.

## The single biggest reason to walk away
**There is no demand security and the margin is structurally squeezed.** SATAT has **no take-or-pay** [F]; the CBO blending mandate sits on CGD entities at the national-aggregate level via a Central Repository Body — it never converts into a guaranteed lift of *your* molecules [F]. Meanwhile SATAT demand has bid feedstock prices up (press mud ₹100→₹500–600/t in ~2 years [F]; NDDB anchoring dung at ₹1/kg [F]), and after the administered ₹77/kg base rate only **~₹500–700 is left per tonne of dung to cover feedstock + debt + profit** — so a dung gate-price past ~₹1,000–1,500/t mathematically erases viability [F/I]. >70% of early Indian CBG plants missed their financial forecasts [F].

## The single condition that would flip it to a "buy"
**M3 only:** if your beverage plant's annual PNG/LPG/furnace-oil spend is large enough that a *right-sized* captive digester — fed by dung + co-substrate available within ~15 km at <₹1,200/t — displaces that fuel at an avoided value that yields **payback < 5 years**, and you underwrite it as a sustainability/energy-cost hedge (not against your 20% growth hurdle). **This requires one number from you: the plant's current annual fuel spend (₹/yr) and unit fuel price** — driver D13, currently a placeholder.

## Opportunity cost (the decisive frame for you)
Your stated mandate is 20%+ CAGR from capital-light, high-conviction equity/commodity bets. A CBG plant is the opposite: **capital-heavy (₹20–25 cr at 5 TPD), operationally punishing (a biological process + feedstock-logistics business needing a full-time operator), illiquid, and sub-WACC in the base case.** Your modelled odds of beating your own hurdle are **7%** [I]. Unless the strategic/ESG value to the FMCG brand is itself the objective, the capital earns more, more safely, in your existing playbook.

## Decision tree (one glance)
1. **Want to SELL gas (M1/M2)?** → No. Below hurdle, no demand security, feedstock-squeezed, ops-heavy. *Walk away* unless you can sign a CGD GSA with ≥50% take-or-pay AND lock co-digestion feedstock <₹1,200/t at ≥10 TPD — then a low-teens-IRR infrastructure asset, still below your hurdle.
2. **Want to cut the beverage plant's fuel bill / build an ESG story (M3)?** → **Investigate.** Get D13 (your fuel spend + price). Right-size a captive digester to your gas demand and locally-available dung. If payback < 5 yr on avoided fuel, it's a defensible cost-hedge. Flex the live `.xlsx` with your real numbers.
3. **Neither true?** → Deploy the capital in your existing 20%+ playbook.
